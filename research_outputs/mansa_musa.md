## I. Executive Summary

Mansa Musa I is widely considered one of the wealthiest individuals in human history. As the ninth Mansa (emperor) of the Mali Empire, he presided over an empire that controlled approximately half of the Old World's gold supply. His famous 1324 pilgrimage to Mecca, during which he distributed so much gold that he crashed the Egyptian gold market for over a decade, remains one of history's most dramatic demonstrations of sovereign wealth.

## II. Variable Analysis

### 1. Population (N): **20,000,000**

**Derivation Logic:**
Population estimates for the Mali Empire vary significantly among scholars:
- Some sources claim up to 50 million at the empire's height
- More conservative estimates place it around 20 million
- The empire covered approximately 1.2 million km² at its peak

I've selected 20 million as a moderate consensus figure, accounting for:
- The empire's vast but sparsely populated Saharan territories
- Dense population centers along the Niger River
- Mansa Musa's own statement to Egyptian officials that his kingdom, though populous, was "like a white birthmark on a black cow" compared to Africa's total population

**Sources:**
- [Mali Empire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Empire): "ruled over a population of around 20 million"
- [Encyclopedia.com](https://www.encyclopedia.com/history/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/mali-empire): Higher estimate of 50 million
- Ibn Khaldun's 14th-century accounts

**Confidence Score:** **Medium**
- No formal census existed
- Estimates based on tax records, settlement patterns, and traveler accounts
- Wide range among scholars (20-50 million)

### 2. Base Output (B): **0.8 oz/person/year**

**Derivation Logic:**

Factors supporting 0.8 oz (slightly below default):

- Limited technological advancement compared to contemporary China or Europe
- Harsh Sahelian climate limited agricultural surplus

### 4. Extraction Margin (M): **0.15**

**Derivation Logic:**
The Mali Empire had a sophisticated three-tiered revenue system:

1. **Trade Taxes:** "The empire taxed every ounce of gold, copper and salt that entered its borders"
2. **Trade Arbitrage:** "bought goods and sold them on at much higher prices"
3. **Natural Resource Control:** Direct ownership of gold mines; "All gold nuggets belonged to the king"
4. **Tribute:** "outlying areas pledging allegiance and giving annual tribute"

The 15% extraction rate accounts for:
- Heavy taxation on the lucrative trans-Saharan trade
- Royal monopoly on gold nuggets (dust could be traded freely)
- Limited ability to tax subsistence farmers directly
- Tribute from vassal states

This is higher than typical light-touch medieval states (5-10%) but lower than total-control empires (25%+) because:
- The gold mines themselves were not directly operated by the state
- Much of the population lived outside direct administrative reach
- Trade taxation was the primary revenue mechanism

**Sources:**
- [Mali Empire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Empire)
- [World History Encyclopedia](https://www.worldhistory.org/Mali_Empire/): "Mali rulers had a triple income"

**Confidence Score:** **Medium**
- Tax rates not precisely recorded
- Structure well-documented but percentages estimated
- Based on comparison with similar medieval trade empires

### 6. Liquid Treasury (L): **16,000,000 oz (500 metric tons equivalent)**

**Derivation Logic:**
This is one of the most debated figures. Evidence includes:

**The 1324 Hajj Evidence:**
- Historian Michael A. Gomez estimates Musa brought **16 metric tons** of gold on his pilgrimage
- This was portable travel wealth, not the entire treasury
- 16 metric tons = 514,413 troy ounces

**Alternative Accounts:**
- 80 camels × 300 lbs each = 24,000 lbs = ~350,000 troy oz (lower estimate)
- "18 tons" rumored for pilgrimage = 579,000 troy oz
- 100 tons claimed in some sources = 3.2 million troy oz

**Treasury Estimation:**
If Musa could afford to take 16 tons on a pilgrimage (and give much of it away), the total treasury reserves were likely significantly larger. Assuming the pilgrimage gold represented 3-5% of total reserves:
- 16 tons ÷ 0.03 = 533 tons total
- Conservative estimate: **500 metric tons = 16,075,350 troy oz**

Rounded to: **16,000,000 oz**

This accounts for:
- Accumulated reserves over "a very long period" as sources note
- Annual gold production of 1-2 tons being stockpiled for generations
- The empire controlling "half of the Old World's gold"

**Sources:**
- [Britannica - Musa I of Mali](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Musa-I-of-Mali): "80 camels, each carrying 300 pounds of gold"
- [World History Encyclopedia](https://www.worldhistory.org/Mansa_Musa_I/): Michael Gomez's 16 metric ton estimate
- [Oxford Case Study](https://globalcapitalism.history.ox.ac.uk/files/ghocmansamusainmalipdf): "Over a very long period Mali had amassed a large reserve of gold"

**Confidence Score:** **Low**
- No treasury inventories survive
- Estimates vary wildly (factor of 10x)
- Based on pilgrimage accounts and extrapolation

### 8. Extraordinary Gains (E): **0 oz**

**Derivation Logic:**
Unlike some conquering empires, Mansa Musa's wealth was primarily:
- Inherited from his predecessor
- Accumulated through ongoing trade taxation
- Not from major one-time pillaging events

The Mali Empire under Musa was largely at peace and consolidated rather than actively conquering and pillaging. While there may have been minor conquests (Gao was annexed), no documented massive one-time wealth injections occurred during his reign comparable to, say, the Spanish conquest of the Aztecs.

**Sources:**
- Historical accounts focus on trade wealth, not conquest plunder
- [World History Encyclopedia](https://www.worldhistory.org/Mansa_Musa_I/)

**Confidence Score:** **High** (absence of evidence for major pillaging)

## IV. Expected Calculation Preview

Using the SAVE formulas:

**Income Valuation:**
- AGR = N × B × V = 20,000,000 × 0.8 × 4.0 = 64,000,000 oz
- AII = AGR × M = 64,000,000 × 0.15 = 9,600,000 oz
- Income Wealth = AII × K = 9,600,000 × 20 = **192,000,000 oz**

**Asset Valuation:**
- Infrastructure = (N × B) × 0.05 = 16,000,000 × 0.05 = 800,000 oz
- Liquid Treasury = 16,000,000 oz
- Asset Wealth = 800,000 + 16,000,000 + 0 + 0 = **16,800,000 oz**

**Total Real Wealth:** 192,000,000 + 16,800,000 = **208,800,000 oz** (~209 million oz)

**Nominal Value (@ $2,900/oz):** ~$605 billion USD
